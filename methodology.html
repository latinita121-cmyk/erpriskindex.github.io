Methodology & Evidence Standards

ERP Risk Index is an evidence-based database that records documented ERP failure outcomes, cost impacts, and risk patterns. It is designed to prioritize defensibility, neutrality, and traceability to primary sources.

Core principle: This platform records outcomes, not intent.

It does not assert fault, misconduct, or deception beyond what is explicitly stated in cited source material.

Evidence Tiers

  • GOLD: Government audit reports, regulators, inspectors general, court records, official filings.
  • SILVER: Tier-1 financial media (e.g., Reuters, WSJ, FT, Bloomberg) used when primary documents are not directly available.
  • EXCLUDED: Vendor marketing pages, unverified blogs, anonymous claims without documentation, opinion content.

Inclusion Rules

  • Every entry must be traceable to a source that meets the evidence tiers above.
  • Entries use neutral language and focus on verifiable facts (costs, dates, outcomes, litigation status).
  • Where a source uses allegations (e.g., in a lawsuit), the database labels them as allegations.
  • Absence from the database does not imply success or failure.

Index Construction (Versioning)

  • The Risk Index summarizes the entries currently included in the database.
  • “Documented cases” means cases included in this database, not all real-world deployments.
  • “Total documented impact” reflects reported costs, write-offs, settlements, or audited uncertainty where documented.
  • Risk bands are descriptive summaries based on documented magnitude and severity, not predictions.

Corrections Policy

  • If a source is corrected or updated, the database will update the entry and note the change date.
  • If a claim cannot be supported by an eligible source, it is removed.

This database records documented outcomes only. It does not assert intent, fault, or misconduct beyond cited sources.
