About the Database

ERP Risk Index is an evidence-based database documenting ERP failure outcomes, cost impacts, and risk patterns using primary sources (government audits, regulators, court records) and Tier-1 financial media where necessary.

What this is: a structured record of documented outcomes.

What this is not: a vendor review site, a product recommendation engine, or a prediction model.

Why this exists

  • ERP programs can create material operational and financial risk.
  • Public documentation is fragmented across audits, filings, and litigation.
  • A centralized, evidence-first record improves accountability and decision quality.

What you can do with it

  • Review documented failure patterns by system and sector.
  • Understand common categories: cost overruns, schedule delays, compliance failures, and abandonment.
  • Use the Risk Index as a structured summary of outcomes included in the database.

Contact

For corrections, additions, or source submissions, use: contact@erpriskindex.com

This database records documented outcomes only. It does not assert intent, fault, or misconduct beyond cited sources.
